- Discovery questions 
  
  1. What is the mission/goal of Aries? (What are we trying to do? What is our motivation? What value are we trying to deliver? What is the problem and how does Aries solve it?)
  2. How do we define and measure success? (What is the goal of this effort? More contributors? More implementations? Bigger implementations?)
     
     1. More implementations and deployments (...+more contributions)
     2. More community involvement
     3. More visibility
  3. Who/what is our target audience? (developers, business decision makers, existing open source contributors, new to open source, new to identity....)
  4. What is the preferred lifecycle for the target audience? (what do we want them to do, in what order, awareness of problem, education, convincing, nudging, contribution, adoption etc...)
     
     1. Awareness: Hyperledger website, Aries Github readme, wiki
     2. Education: Explainer videos, demonstrations, industries, blog posts (community contributed information)
  5. What are the friction/pain points of Aries? (documentation, understanding, visibility, code issues etc?)
     
     1. Misconceptions of 'too hard'
     2. How to get seat at table, from dev-exec..., easiest way in, not approachable
     3. Examples of use, how to see how big it is
     4. How to get in
  6. What types of actions should we consider? (new website, clean up wiki, media placements, content, blog, announcement, demos recordings etc...)
     
     1. Website
     2. Github
     3. Wiki
  7. Who/what are our competitors?
